PostDoc

Bao Truong

Bao is a postdoc in the junior research group “Computational Social Science”. Her research focuses on the development of technological and policy interventions to reduce online harms. These include: methods to detect abuse of social media, tools to evaluate platform policies and governance, and design heuristics that promote social cohesion and well-being. She approaches these questions using quantitative methods, studying large-scale datasets, network analysis, natural language processing, and machine learning.

Interests

  • Online harms and safety
  • Platform governance and digital policy
  • Social media and abuse detection
  • Network analysis
  • Natural language processing
  • Machine learning

Education

  • 2025 Ph.D. in Informatics (Complex Networks and Systems track), Indiana University Bloomington, USA
  • 2018 B.Sc. in Technological Systems Management, Stony Brook University, USA
